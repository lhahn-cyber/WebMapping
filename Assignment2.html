Hetchy Hetchy Reservoir:

Hetch Hetchy Reservoir is the primary water source for San Francisco. It's located in Hetch Hetchy Valley in Yosemite, and was the subject of an environmental debate in the early 1900s over whether to conserve the valley, or dam it (flooding the valley) to provide water to the city. The O'Shaughnessy Dam was completed in 1923, and is still ocassionally a subject of controversy.

Kirkwood Hydroelectric Plant:

Some of the water from the reservoir feeds this hydroelectric plant, which is of 3 in the area that help provide the city of San Francisco with power. It began operating in 1967 and it's small size allows it to be considered renewable under the RPS in California. About 16% of the city's power supply comes from non-large hydropower renewables.

San Francisco:

Despite it being all downhill travel, it takes about 3 days for the water to travel 167 miles to the city. It goes through a series of tunnels, indluding those of the hydroelectric powerhouses, and then the Tesla Ultraviolet Treatment Facility before it reaches San Francisco, where it serves about 2.7 million residents and businesses.
